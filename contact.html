About me

My journey to design

When I went to a performing arts elementary school, I remember getting scolded by my parents for wasting ink from our printer. I had written a script that I was planning on convincing other students to perform. Admittedly, it wasn't good. It was a watered-down version of the Christmas Carol. I was 6.

I... acquired after effects as a kid and started learning compositing and animation. In middle school, I took a strong interest in claymation. I spent days working on 4 minutes clips of these nonsensical animated stop motions. When youtube came out, I jumped on movie maker and started recording and editing videos.

So much of my life and what I focused on centered around creative expression. But I had always felt a scratching desire for something more concrete. I learned coding fundamentals in middle school and went more in-depth in high school. I spent hours on problems and bugs and things that didn't work. These were my favorite times during coding because it felt like a puzzle with a correct solution. The existence of a correct answer is what I have always been looking for in my creative pursuit.

What attracted me to UX was the UI. But as I learned about research and testing and iteration. It was like a melding of these two worlds that I had always seen as opposites. I fell in love with design so profoundly. I can't see myself doing something else.

I'm Henry, and I'm a designer. I don't know if that was cheesy, but it's true.
